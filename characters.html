The Story So Far

Beneath the fractured sky of Ovener lie the Four Great Realms: the war-scarred Duchy of Malric, the mist-veiled Sisterhood of the Shroud, the arcane-spined Tower-Cities of Vel Thalor, and the lawless expanse known only as The Wastes. Each is locked in an uneasy dance—treaties stitched with distrust, alliances traded like coin, and omens gathering like crows on the battlements.

In the north, Archduke Malric has declared himself Sovereign Regent, fracturing the old High Council and pressing his claim with steel and shadow. His chief operative is Baron Redmar, a ruthless warlord with noble blood but no love for diplomacy. Captain Branth, once their loyal enforcer, has since broken ranks, forming a mercenary cohort that defends the free peoples along the borderlands—if the price is right.

Beyond the veil of faith, Sister Elenwen wanders from ruin to ruin, healing wounds and whispering forgotten names of gods no longer worshiped. She is watched—always—from afar by Inquisitor Vale, whose zealotry burns brighter than any scripture. And somewhere between prophecy and madness, Madame Nyla speaks riddles that shift each time the wind turns.

Far to the west, the ancient orc tribes rally behind Gruk the Unyielding, who seeks to unite the old clans beneath one banner—not for conquest, but for survival. His emissary, Faelan Duskwatch, has already begun negotiating a fragile accord with Lady Seraphine, the enigmatic patron whose past may hold the key to peace... or catastrophe. Hovering over it all is High Arcanist Volmir, a fugitive from Vel Thalor whose grasp of forbidden magic threatens to tear open reality itself.

And through the cracks in these alliances, Jinx Whistletoe skips—laughing, listening, and always knowing more than she lets on.
